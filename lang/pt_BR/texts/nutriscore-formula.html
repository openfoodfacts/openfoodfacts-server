Pontuação Nutricional e de Cor da Nutri-Score

A system of grades from A to E to simplify nutritional labeling

In the report "Proposals for a new impetus to the French public health nutrition policy in the framework of the National Health Strategy" (pdf), Professor Serge Hercberg advocates for the adoption of a system of grades from A to E on the front of food to allow to simply compare the nutritional quality of products.

Essas cores são definidas pelo cálculo de uma pontuação nutricional que reflete para a primeira parte da energia, Gorduras saturadas, açúcares, sódio (níveis altos são considerados prejudiciais), e pela segunda parte da proporção de frutas, legumes e castanhas, fibras e proteínas (altos níveis são considerados bons para a saúde).

The nutritional score on Open Food Facts

The formula for calculating the nutritional score, the thresholds of the grades and the various tweaking proposals were transmitted by Professor Hercberg's team. This formula was the subject of studies and adaptations for the French market.

A versão adoptada oficialmente e implementada sobre o Open Food Facts é a do relatório AVISO do Alto Conselho para a Saúde Pública relacionado às informações sobre a qualidade nutricional dos produtos alimentares publicadas em 25 de junho 2015.

However, we calculated the nutritional score for products sold on French soil and referenced in the database Open Food Facts, and determined the corresponding A to E grades. This allows in particular to compare the nutritional score and / or notes of different products, and to study the distribution for each product category.

Formula to calculate the nutritional score

Points are given to the products according to the amount of nutrients they contain per 100 g.

São dados pontos A para os nutrientes considerados "ruins" e pontos C são dados para os nutrientes "bons" e o conteúdo em frutas, vegetais e nozes.

Pontos A são a soma de pontos para energia, gordura saturada, açúcar e sódio.

The C points are the sum of points for fruits, vegetables and nuts, for fiber and protein.

Note: potatoes, sweet potatoes, taro, cassava and tapioca are not included in fruits, vegetables and nuts.

Nutritional score calculation

  • If A points < 11 then score = A points - C points
  • If A points ≥ 11
    • If the points for fruit, vegetables and nuts = 5, then score = A points - C points
    • If the points for fruit, vegetables and nuts < 5, then score = A points - (fiber points + points for points fruits, vegetables and nuts)

Adaptations for the French market

A fórmula da pontuação nutricional indicada acima corresponde à fórmula da pontuação nutricional da Food Standards Agency (FSA) no Reino Unido.

A equipe do professor Hercberg oferece adaptações para o mercado francês que são implementadas para calcular a pontuação no Open Food Facts.

Adaptation for cheese

For cheeses, points for the proteins are substracted from the score even if the A points are ≥ 11.

Adaptation for fat

Para ajudar a diferenciar gorduras (óleos, margarinas, crèmes fraîches), todos os que têm taxas de gordura saturada mais altas que o limite máximo de 10 g, outra grade que leva em conta a proporção de ácidos graxos saturados na gordura total é aplicada:

Adaptations for fruits and vegetables

The nuts are not taken into account for the calculation of the content of fruits and vegetables.

Limites de notas Nutri-Score

Os limites usados ​​para atribuir as notas de A a E são:

Solid Foods:

  • A: até -1
  • B: de 0 a 2
  • C: de 3 a 10
  • D: de 11 a 18
  • E: acima de 19

Beverages:

Only water (mineral and spring, with the exclusion of flavored waters) is graded A.

  • A: águas minerais e águas de nascente
  • B: até 1
  • C: de 2 a 5
  • D: de 6 a 9
  • E: acima de 10

Warning: This score may still not match the recommended score:

  • The fiber content is not necessarily present in the nutrition table. When the latter is not indicated, any positive contribution of the fibers is not taken into account. Nós encorajamos todos os produtores a indicar o conteúdo da fibra de seus produtos na embalagem dos mesmos.

  • The points corresponding to the levels in fruits and vegetables are not taken into account for all products. The fruit , vegetables content is indeed not on the package within the nutrition table, and we'll have to determine it with other information (such as the words "minimum fruit content", the category of products (eg. jams contain 50% fruit) and/or the ingredient list.)
    • A new field "Fruits, vegetables (minimum)" was created to record the values of the terms "minimum content of fruit / vegetables" etc.
    • By default, the products in certain categories are considered to have a minimum content of:
      • 100%: juice
      • 90%: stewed fruits
      • 85%: vegetables
      • 50%: jams
      • other categories to define
      To calculate the nutritional score, it is important to distinguish levels above 40%, 60% and 80%.
  • For the lyophilized food (packaged soups, etc.), the score should be calculated for the reconstituted product, not the raw product as is currently the case.
