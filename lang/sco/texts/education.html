Food for thought and education

Educators love reusing our data and apps to engage the public on nutrition or on other topics (like learning to code).

Education is one of the core pillars of Open Food Facts. Educators love reusing our data and apps to engage the public on nutrition or on other topics (like learning to code). We also love to engage with educators to see what they can come up with, and how we can help them.

If you're using Open Food Facts as part of an education project, please let us know at contact@openfoodfacts.org

Teaching students how to code

Open Classrooms, the #1 MOOC platform in France, uses Open Food Facts to teach students how to plan, manage and code a fully functionnal project called PurBeurre, as well as code in Python.

Teaching students how to code

Simplon is providing economic opportunities to all by training them to tech. They use Open Food Facts to teach students how to code a fun project to get more information about your food.

Training the next generation of data scientists

Aspiring data scientists use Open Food Facts on Kaggle to hone and showcase their skills, all while playing with food. Kaggle has selected Open Food Facts as a featured dataset.

Raising nutrition issues in uncommon places

There are vending machines in schools, hospitals, public services, at work. Usually there's no other option, and the products proposed are questionable. What if we augmented reality and sparkled a debate on Twitter by overlaying the Nutri-Score ? #NutriScoreYourVendingMachine

Using tablets and Open Food Facts to answer nutrition questions

One of the activities proposed on iPad is benchmarking sodas using the web version of Open Food Facts.

Engaging all audiences

We make many interventions in schools, forums, libraries because we're not just an app.

Countering lobby influence by helping create science-based school kits

Educational toolkits should not be written by CEDUS or CNIEL, but by public science organisations.

How much sugar?

Can you guess the equivalent amount of sugar cubes in your food? Try How much sugar? to find out.

Food Archeology

What was food like 10 years, 20 years, 100 years ago ? Our food archeology project tries to find out. Do not hesitate to add older products from your attic in Open Food Facts.
