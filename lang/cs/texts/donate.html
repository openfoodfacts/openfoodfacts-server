A collaborative, free and open database of food products with an increasing impact

Launched in 2012, the Open Food Facts project continues to grow: in 2018, our collaborative, free and open food product database has almost doubled in size, having already quadrupled in 2017! This database is reused for free by over 100 mobile apps and web services and is used by public health research teams.

As mentioned in our last activity report, this very rapid growth in the number of products and reuses opens up new perspectives and opportunities, but also presents new challenges that we now need to address.

Human resources at the service of a citizen project

Since 2012, the Open Food Facts project has been entirely run by volunteer citizens grouped together in a 1901 non-profit association. The project is based on citizen gathering (crowdsourcing) to feed the database (on the model of other citizen projects like OpenStreetMap and Wikipedia), and also on volunteers for management and technical development.

Open Food Facts has a growing impact, but we also have growing difficulties to cope with the extra work this growth entails. To sustain and extend our action, we will put in place human resources dedicated to the project, who can work there full time, being paid. 

This will enable a continued growth of the database and will enable addressing the challenges we have identified, and it will also extend the project into new directions (such as extending to new types of products like cosmetics with Open Beauty Facts), or the creation of new services and applications (eg in the field of the fight against waste) etc.
