A first step to contribute to Open Food Facts is to present it to you friends and family.
You can get on Twitter, Facebook or Google Plus and ask a few friends and family to join the project.

Why present Open Food Facts ?

Open Food Facts relies on citizen crowdsourcing


25 000 contributors have added 850 000 products on Open Food Facts. The database includes products from more than 100 different countries, but the bulk of them comes from France - where Open Food Facts was launched in 2012 - and from a few countries like Spain, the UK, the US and Belgium where we have some very motivated contributors. We need to change that: we need to add products from your country and try to reach a critical mass of products. Of even greater importance, We need to create and develop strong local communities of contributors.

Creating local communities

To create this community, we need people who can present Open Food Facts in front of as many different audiences as possible, from the most obvious ones to the most unlikely. In Frankrijk hebben we alle uitnodigingen aanvaard om ons project te presenteren, en elke keer hebben we snel een band met de mensen die onze presentaties hebben bijgewoond. Voedsel is een universeel onderwerp waar iedereen zich mee bezighoudt. Explaining how we can better understand labels and easily compare products always generates a lot of interest and enthusiasm.

Open Food Facts presenteren is makkelijk.

Het is makkelijk. We already have ready-made LibreOffice, Powerpoint presentations you can translate and adapt into your language. We also have transcripts and videos of people presenting Open Food Facts at various events.

Handling Q&A after your presentation

People usually ask many questions because the project and your presentation was thought-provoking. Maak je niet teveel zorgen:
  • Er is een pagina met de veelgestelde vragen over het project. Dat zijn de vragen die steeds weer worden gesteld en die 90 procent van alle gesteld vragen moet dekken
  • Zo niet, dan is het altijd mogelijk om dingen te zeggen als: "Ik weet het niet zeker, maar ik denk dat…", "Ik heb geen antwoord, maar kom alsjeblieft naar me toe na de Q&A, en ik zal ervoor zorgen dat je een antwoord krijgt"

Use the Presentation Kit to prepare your presentation.

Just download the presentation kit from the wiki. If it's not translated, you just have to take some time to translate it in your language. It's rather short, and it's a good way to memorize the presentation :-)
Don't forget to upload the translated version back to the wiki.
Don't forget to ask advice on Slack, as well as tips to print stickers and presentation material.
